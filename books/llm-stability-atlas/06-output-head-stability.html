Chapter 6

Output-Head Stability

The output head is an unusually important failure surface because the rest of the model can appear numerically ordinary while the LM logits drift into a bad regime. Public large-scale evidence still favors z-loss as the default production control, while output embedding centering remains a promising but less mature frontier direction. PaLM OEC Small-Scale Proxies

Why This Chapter Exists

Many stability recipes stop at clipping and attention controls. That is incomplete. The LM head maps [B, T, D] → [B, T, V], and that final projection can drift even when the trunk still looks stable by coarse metrics. This chapter exists to make the output-head problem explicit rather than treating it as a vague “logits got large” anecdote.

Intuition

The core intuition is that softmax does not only care about relative ordering. The normalizer itself can become numerically dangerous. If logsumexp(logits) grows too aggressively, then the model may become hard to optimize even before every other diagnostic signal is obviously broken.

Formal Mechanics

PaLM’s public stabilization term is: PaLM

L_z = λ_z · log² Z

Z = Σ_v exp(L_v)

λ_z = 1e-4

This is not intended to improve generation style directly. Its role is to keep the softmax normalizer controlled. OEC instead argues that output embedding anisotropy is a deeper cause and proposes a centering-based approach. That theoretical move is interesting, but the large-scale evidence base in this bundle remains thinner than for z-loss.

Example

Suppose a run shows acceptable loss and no obvious attention instability, but logsumexp(logits) rises steadily over time. That signal points much more directly to the output head than to the residual stream. In that situation the pragmatic move is not “clip harder” but “add or verify z-loss, then monitor whether the LM-head statistics stabilize.”

Strengths

  • z-loss has direct public precedent at frontier scale.
  • The control targets a failure that clipping does not localize well.
  • The required monitoring variable, logsumexp(logits), is straightforward to instrument.

Limitations And Failure Cases

  • z-loss is a stabilizer, not a full causal explanation of output embedding geometry.
  • OEC is currently a cautious frontier direction here, not a public default recommendation.
  • An output-head symptom may still co-occur with attention or numeric-path failures, so the metric story must be read in context.

Practical Notes

If a team needs a production-default answer today, use z-loss. If the team is running controlled experiments and believes LM-head geometry is still the limiting problem, OEC is worth evaluating, but that should be framed as research work, not as a settled recipe replacement. Also keep the distinction between attention soft-capping and final-logit soft-capping clear: they are related ideas applied to different tensors. PaLM OEC Gemma 2 docs
