Chapter 1

Failure Taxonomy

This chapter argues that most discussion of “LLM training instability” fails because it treats unlike failures as if they were one failure. Public evidence supports a more useful split: residual and normalization geometry, attention-logit collapse, output-head divergence, numeric-path failures, and repetition that may be decode-side rather than train-side. Xiong 2020 Entropy Collapse Holtzman

Why This Chapter Exists

The first operational mistake in a bad run is often a classification mistake. If a model spikes late because a few heads sharpen until attention entropy collapses, then lowering the learning rate or clipping harder may buy time without fixing the cause. If the model repeats tokens at generation time, then modifying the optimizer may be completely irrelevant if the failure is decode induced. The purpose of this chapter is therefore diagnostic: it defines the mechanism families that the rest of the handbook will analyze separately.

Intuition

A causal LM training run can fail in at least three broad places: inside the residual stream [B, T, D], inside the distributions derived from that stream, especially attention logits and output logits, or outside the core backbone in the numeric path and decoding path. The key distinction is that gradients, losses, entropy, and generated text are not interchangeable observables. A healthy total loss can hide a bad LM head. Good greedy outputs can hide a model that only looks sane under one decoder setting.

Mechanism Families

1. Residual and normalization geometry

This family explains early divergence under aggressive learning rates and some late depth-driven amplification. Xiong et al. make the classic argument: Post-LN yields large expected gradients near the output layer at initialization, whereas Pre-LN makes the gradient field much better behaved. Xiong 2020

2. Attention-logit collapse

This family explains the run that looks healthy for a long time and then suddenly spikes after one or a few heads become too sharp. The public entropy-collapse literature is especially useful here because it ties the instability to a measurable precursor: decreasing attention entropy. Entropy Collapse Small-Scale Proxies

3. Output-head divergence

This family explains the case where the decoder trunk is not yet obviously broken but the softmax normalizer or output embedding geometry drifts into a numerically pathological regime. z-loss is the strongest public production anchor in this family.

4. Numeric-path failures

This family covers precision, reduction dtype, optimizer-state dtype, and checkpoint recovery policy. These are often treated as “engineering details,” but large runs repeatedly show that they are part of the stability stack, not an implementation footnote.

5. Repetition and degeneration

This family is distinct because repetition can emerge even when the training dynamics are otherwise stable. Holtzman et al. show that decode-time likelihood maximization can itself induce degenerate loops. Welleck et al. show that train-time unlikelihood can reduce repeated strings directly. The taxonomy therefore ends by separating model instability from search instability. Holtzman Welleck

Strengths

  • The taxonomy prevents the common error of using one mitigation for every symptom.
  • It gives a direct bridge from observed metrics to concrete chapters and interventions.
  • It aligns with public source anchors rather than private folklore: Xiong 2020, PaLM, GLM-130B, the entropy-collapse paper, Holtzman et al., and Welleck et al.

Limitations And Failure Cases

  • Some real runs involve more than one mechanism family at once; the taxonomy is a diagnostic simplification, not a guarantee of clean separation.
  • Frontier optimizer-state behavior can blur the line between architecture-side and optimizer-side failures.
  • For omni models, modality-balancing losses may introduce additional instability axes not fully captured here. [MISSING FROM SOURCE]

Practical Notes

If you do not yet know which family you are in, do not begin by adding a new loss term. Start by logging the minimum diagnosis set: global grad norm, embedding-layer grad norm, attention entropy per head, max attention logit, and logsumexp statistics for the LM head. That metric set is the shortest path from a vague failure report to a chapter-specific intervention. Small-Scale Proxies Entropy Collapse

Evidence Notes

PaLM is the strongest public reminder that even a carefully engineered large run can still spike despite grad_clip = 1.0 and z-loss. The taxonomy matters precisely because a generic “stability recipe” was not enough to erase all failure modes at frontier scale.
