Chapter 8

Output-Head Stability

This chapter separates LM-head divergence from the rest of the stability story. The rerun keeps z-loss as the battle-tested default and places OEC in the frontier margin because the public precedent for z-loss remains materially stronger. PaLM Small-Scale Proxies OEC

Why This Chapter Exists

A run can look mostly healthy until the output layer ceases to be numerically sane. If that possibility is ignored, teams often over-attribute the problem to the trunk or to generic optimizer behavior. This chapter exists to keep the LM head visible as its own control surface.

Mechanism

The final hidden states H_L [B, T, D] are projected to logits L [B, T, V]. A useful practical statistic is the softmax normalizer Z = \sum_v e^{L_v}, or more stably its log-domain form logsumexp(logits). If log Z drifts into an unhealthy regime, the LM head can become the most unstable part of the model even when the rest of the network still seems ordinary.

z-loss As The Default

PaLM gives the strongest public production anchor in this chapter because it names both the form and the coefficient: 

z-loss = 1e-4 * log^2 Z

That level of specificity matters. The reason this book treats z-loss as the default is not only that the mechanism is plausible. It is that a flagship public run used it at scale and exposed the exact coefficient. PaLM

OEC As The Frontier Challenger

Output Embedding Centering is promising because it argues that the deeper issue lies in the geometry of the output embeddings rather than only in the normalizer magnitude. That is a meaningful conceptual move. The reason the chapter still keeps OEC at partial maturity is evidential, not ideological: the public large-scale text-model precedent remains thinner than PaLM’s z-loss precedent. OEC

Diagnostic Branch

If attention entropy is quiet but logsumexp(logits) climbs, the LM head becomes the most plausible failure surface. That is the moment to add or verify z-loss. If the baseline stack already includes z-loss and the output head still appears to dominate failure, only then does an OEC-style experiment become coherent.

Why This Is Not Just Attention Or Numeric Hygiene Again

Output-head instability often arrives late enough that it is tempting to blame whichever upstream chapter was most recently discussed. That is exactly the confusion this section is meant to dissolve. If attention entropy is stable, the score path may not be the problem. If dtype paths are already conservative and the run is not producing non-finite values, Chapter 5 may no longer be the best explanatory frame. The point of this chapter is that the final projection and normalizer can remain a distinct source of instability even after earlier chapters have been handled responsibly.

Anti-Patterns

  • Assuming large logits are always downstream reflections of attention failure rather than a head-local problem.
  • Using decode-side penalties to “fix” a model whose training-time output logits are actually diverging.
  • Promoting OEC to the default stack without matched public maturity.

Strengths And Limitations

Strength. The chapter explains why a run can look healthy until the very end of the model becomes numerically pathological, and it has an exact public default.

Limitation. The bundle does not yet support treating OEC as equally mature. That restraint is deliberate and comes from evidence sufficiency, not aesthetic conservatism.

Practical Notes

Add z-loss before experimenting with OEC. Only move to OEC when the baseline recipe is already stable and the output-head failure still appears to be the bottleneck.
