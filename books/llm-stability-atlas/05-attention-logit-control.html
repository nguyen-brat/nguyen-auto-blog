Chapter 5

Attention-Logit Control

Some of the most frustrating LM failures are late and abrupt: training looks healthy, then a few heads sharpen, entropy falls, and the run becomes unstable. This chapter explains why attention logits are a distinct control surface and compares the three most relevant public families: Q/K normalization, soft-capping, and sigmaReparam. Entropy Collapse OLMo 2 docs Gemma 2 docs

Why This Chapter Exists

Late spikes are often misread as “optimizer weirdness.” The attention-entropy literature offers a sharper explanation: if attention logits grow too large, softmax becomes too peaked and the training dynamics can destabilize quickly. This chapter exists to explain why constraining the logits before softmax is often cleaner than only reacting after bad gradients appear.

Intuition

Attention builds a distribution from pairwise scores. If those scores become extreme, the distribution becomes too sharp, a small number of positions dominate, entropy collapses, and gradients can become brittle. Attention-logit controls all attack the same place: the tensor S ∈ ℝ^[B, H, T, T] before the softmax.

Formal Mechanics

In a standard decoder block,

S = (QKᵀ / √d_h) + M

where Q, K ∈ ℝ^[B, H, T, d_h] and M is the causal mask. Three intervention families are most relevant:

  • Q/K normalization: normalize Q and K before the dot product, as documented for OLMo 2.
  • Soft-capping: transform logits with c · tanh(S / c), as exposed in Gemma 2 docs.
  • sigmaReparam: use spectral-normalized reparameterization to discourage entropy collapse.

Example

Soft-capping is the easiest concrete example because the public numbers are explicit. Gemma 2 exposes: Gemma 2 docs Gemma 2 blog

attn_logit_softcapping = 50.0

final_logit_softcapping = 30.0

This gives a simple engineering story: small logits pass almost linearly, but very large ones are bounded before they create extreme softmax sharpness. Q/K normalization pursues a similar practical end with a lighter structural change.

Strengths

  • These methods target a concrete precursor variable rather than a downstream symptom.
  • Q/K normalization is especially lightweight for modern decoder stacks.
  • Soft-capping provides an explicit magnitude bound that is easy to explain and instrument.

Limitations And Failure Cases

  • No single public benchmark in this bundle cleanly ranks Q/K normalization, soft-capping, and sigmaReparam on the same dense LM backbone.
  • These controls do not solve output-head drift or numeric-path fragility by themselves.
  • Some implementations may complicate kernel paths or add extra elementwise overhead.

Practical Notes

Q/K normalization is the easiest default if attention metrics show drift and you want a minimal architectural change. Soft-capping is attractive when you want an explicit bound and already have a kernel path that tolerates it. sigmaReparam is the most theory-heavy option and may be more attractive when the goal is to study entropy behavior directly rather than only patch a production training stack. Evidence Notes

The strongest public conceptual anchor remains the attention entropy-collapse paper, while the strongest open implementation anchor for a current LM recipe is OLMo 2 documentation. That is enough to justify this chapter, but not enough to claim one universal winner across all scales and contexts.
