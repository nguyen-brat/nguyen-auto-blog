Chapter 7

Attention-Logit Control

This chapter treats attention entropy collapse as a distinct precursor and compares Q/K normalization, soft-capping, and sigmaReparam as one family of controls instead of isolated tricks. Entropy Collapse OLMo2 Docs Gemma2 Docs Gemma2 Blog

Why This Chapter Exists

Attention failures are often misread as generic late spikes because the visible divergence arrives only after the underlying score distribution has already become too sharp. This chapter exists to make that hidden precursor explicit and to show why the right intervention family is often attention-specific rather than global.

Mechanism

In standard self-attention, queries and keys produce scores whose scale determines how peaked the softmax distribution becomes. When a few heads become excessively sharp, attention entropy collapses and the run can transition abruptly from ordinary behavior to instability. The Apple paper is the strongest public mechanism source here because it does not merely report spikes; it links them to entropy collapse and motivates controls that constrain the score path itself. Entropy Collapse

The Control Family

Q/K normalization

Q/K normalization is the lightest deployment-oriented move in the public open-model stack. OLMo2 applies RMSNorm to queries and keys before the dot product, which directly constrains attention-score scale without changing the rest of the training recipe dramatically. OLMo2 Docs

Soft-capping

Gemma 2 exposes attention-logit soft-capping explicitly. The main attraction is that the transform is bounded while remaining approximately linear for smaller logits. This is appealing operationally because it offers a direct ceiling on runaway magnitude. Gemma2 Docs Gemma2 Blog

sigmaReparam

The Apple paper gives the strongest theory-first framing through spectral normalization plus a learned scalar. In practice, this makes sigmaReparam the most mechanism-rich option in the family, though it is less visibly represented in mainstream open decoder-LM configs than Q/K norm or soft-capping.

First-Line Fixes And Escalation

If a run is stable for a long interval and then spikes abruptly, inspect attention entropy before changing the entire optimizer stack. If entropy collapse is visible, the first-line move is to add Q/K normalization or soft-capping if neither is present. If the model already has one of these and still shows collapse, then a theory-heavier intervention like sigmaReparam or a deeper architectural review becomes more justified. The important thing is that the escalation remains within the same causal family first.

Why The Book Does Not Declare A Single Winner

The rerun contradiction log matters here. Apple emphasizes sigmaReparam; OLMo2 exposes Q/K normalization; Gemma 2 exposes soft-capping. What the evidence base does not currently contain is a clean same-backbone public ablation that ranks these methods decisively. The correct presentation is therefore a family comparison, not a winner-take-all claim.

Anti-Patterns

  • Waiting until loss explodes instead of watching entropy-like precursors.
  • Treating attention collapse as generic optimizer failure without touching the score path.
  • Claiming one public method universally dominates when the comparison base is still qualitative.

Strengths And Limitations

Strength. This chapter has the cleanest precursor metric and the clearest direct mechanism in the whole handbook.

Limitation. The lack of a same-backbone public head-to-head ablation means comparisons remain qualitative rather than final.

Practical Notes

In the current evidence base, Q/K normalization is the lightest entry point, soft-capping is the clearest bounded option, and sigmaReparam is the strongest theory-first control. Which one should come first in a concrete codebase is therefore an engineering-choice question, not a settled universal ranking question.
